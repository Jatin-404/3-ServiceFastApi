The sun rose slowly over the mountains casting long shadows across the valley below. Birds began to sing their morning songs filling the air with a symphony of sound. The farmer woke early as he always did walking out to survey his fields before breakfast. His crops were growing well this season and he felt a quiet satisfaction seeing the green rows stretching out before him. He had worked this land for thirty years learning its moods and rhythms like an old friend. Some years were hard with drought or frost stealing away months of effort. But this year the rains had come at the right time and the soil was rich and dark. His daughter came out to join him handing him a cup of coffee without saying a word. They stood together watching the morning light spread across the fields. She was leaving for university next week and he was trying not to think about how quiet the house would become. She had always been curious asking questions about everything from why the sky was blue to how seeds knew which way was up. He had not always had the answers but he had always listened and that seemed to matter more. The old dog padded out from the barn and sat beside them wagging its tail slowly. In the distance a tractor engine started up on a neighboring farm breaking the morning silence. The farmer finished his coffee handed the cup back to his daughter and walked toward the fields to begin another day.
